Description:

a classic American dish featuring chicken pieces, typically soaked in a seasoned buttermilk marinade to add moisture and tenderize the meat. The chicken is then dredged in heavily seasoned flour (often including garlic powder, paprika, and cayenne) and fried, traditionally in a cast-iron skillet with lard or oil, until it has a golden-brown, crispy exterior.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or peanut oil)

Steps:

  1. Marinate chicken in buttermilk for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix flour, salt, paprika, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper.
  3. Dredge each chicken piece in the seasoned flour mixture.
  4. Heat oil in a deep skillet or fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Fry chicken pieces until golden brown and cooked through (about 12-15 minutes).
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.
